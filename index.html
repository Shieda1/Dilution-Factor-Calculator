What is the dilution factor?
The dilution ratio is the ratio of solute to solvent and it is often used for simple dilutions, one in which a unit volume of a liquid material is combined with an appropriate volume of a solvent liquid to achieve the desired concentration.
How to calculate dilution factor?
To calculate dilution factor you should use the following formula:
dilution factor = final solution volume / volume from stock solution
Example: 500 mL of a stock solution is diluted with solvent / diluent to a final volume of 4500 mL, calculate the dilution factor?
dilution factor = final solution volume / volume from stock solution
dilution factor = 4500 / 500
dilution factor = 9
Why use dilution factor?
The dilution factor is used to express how much of the original stock solution is present in the total solution, after dilution.
